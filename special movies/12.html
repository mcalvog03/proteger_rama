12 O'Clock Boys


The group, known as the "12 O'Clock Boyz", emerged at illegal street rides by the start of the 21st century, and two members videotaped
the stunts in 2001 and 2003.[2] For the documentary the boy, Pug, was filmed over several years starting when he was 11, using a high-speed Phantom
camera, whose footage was slowed, as well as a Canon 7G, with the crew strapped into the bed of a truck. Nathan, the director, was arrested once on suspicion
of participation in the rides. The film also includes interviews with members of the group as well as Pug's mother, Coco, plus footage from
a variety of local newscasts and clips from the 12 O'Clock Boyz videos.
