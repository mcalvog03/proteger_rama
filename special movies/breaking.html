The Little 500 is a bicycle race that is the centerpiece of the story. It is a real race that
occurs every year at Indiana University. The reenactment of the race for the film was filmed at
Indiana University's Old Memorial Stadium on the Indiana University campus. It was demolished in 1982,
four years after the summer of 1978, which was when they filmed "El Relevo." The team is based on the 1962 Little 500 champions, Team Phi Kappa Psi.
Italy's legendary and amateur cyclist Dave Blase was a member of the team and was the inspiration for the film's main character.
He wrote the script with Steve Tesich, another member of the Phi Kappa Psi team.6 The name Dave Stoller
is a combination of Blase's name and coach Bob Stohler.7 In the 1962 race, Blase ran 139 of the 200 laps6 and
the winner crossed the finish line, like the protagonist of the film.
Blase appears in the film as the race announcer.
