Bicycle Thief


<

The Bicycle Thief is a 1948 Italian neorealist drama film directed by Vittorio De Sica. It follows the story of a poor father searching
in post-World War II Rome for his stolen bicycle, without which he will lose the job which was to be the salvation of
his young family. Adapted for the screen by Cesare Zavattini from the 1946 novel by Luigi Bartolini, and starring Lamberto Maggiorani as
the desperate father and Enzo Staiola as his plucky young son, Bicycle Thieves received an
Academy Honorary Award (most outstanding foreign language film) in 1950, and in 1952 was deemed
the greatest film of all time by Sight & Sound magazine's poll of filmmakers and critics
